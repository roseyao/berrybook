# The Everything Treasure — Story & Prompts  (DRAFT)

**Status:** DRAFT for review — not yet wired into `App.js`. **STORY FIRST** — no art yet.
**id:** `everything-treasure` · **slot:** book 10 · images → `public/images/book10/`
**Working title:** *Ivy & Oliver: The Everything Treasure*

**Theme / lesson:** Stopping the "NOT FAIR" complaints and learning to feel **genuine joy for someone else's good fortune**. The curse: whining about others' treasure makes your own worthless. The antidote: celebrating instead of comparing — joy shared grows stronger, not weaker.

**Cast & creatures:**
- **Recurring (use shipped refs):** Ivy (unicorn — white, purple mane, wings, star birthmark), Oliver (dragon — emerald green, orange patches, orange heart chest-patch, small rounded horns), Mom (adult unicorn — golden honey coat, lavender mane, gold heart necklace), Dad (adult dragon — bronzed-green, silver patches, soft-bodied, small rounded horns), Hazel (newborn pink unicorn, rose mane, gold-glowing horn nub).
- **New main character:** **Captain Grumble-Beard** — a grizzled BEAR pirate (big, gruff, weather-beaten brown fur with a few patches of grey, salty pirate hat, eye patch, ratty old captain's coat draped over his back, a long messy grey "beard" of fur under his chin). Starts surrounded by **dull, lifeless treasures** that hang off him like he's wearing his complaints; **transforms into gleaming-bright** treasures by the end as the curse breaks. Surprisingly gentle once kids admit the truth.
- **Minor scene characters (no refs needed — describe inline):**
  - **Princess Pout** — a young animal princess (small fox or sheep in a tiny tarnished crown), surrounded by gray joyless toys.
  - **Duke Grumble** — a large grumpy bear in a noble's collar at a feast that looks gray and tasteless.
  - **Count Compare** — a tall giraffe in a long vest, in a room of dull possessions.

**New world asset:** **The Complaint Kingdom** — a gloomy gray stone village/castle complex under perpetually overcast skies; everything gray and joyless until the curse breaks, then brilliant golden color floods back. (Save ref to `content/world/refs/complaint-kingdom.png`.)

### Character growth goals
- **Ivy + Oliver together (jointly):** Recognize their own constant "NOT FAIR" whining mirrored back at them; learn to feel real, generous joy at the other's good thing (cookie, popsicle, screen time, story). They both grow on the same arc, which is unusual in this series.

## scene6 — Breaking the Curse, Finding the Real Treasure
**image:** `/Images/book10/6.png`

### Text
"Let's TRY," Ivy said. "Really try."
They practiced right there in the gray town square.
When Oliver found a tarnished golden coin in the cobblestones, instead of wanting one too, Ivy cheered: "WOW! That's the SHINIEST one!" The coin blazed with light — and a matching one appeared at Ivy's hooves.
When Ivy found a crystal that hummed a tiny song, Oliver said: "That's SO cool! Play us a tune!" The crystal's music grew sweeter, and a tiny harmonizing crystal appeared at Oliver's feet.
"It's WORKING!" they laughed.
They spent the whole afternoon helping the kingdom. Princess Pout learned to say "I'm happy for YOU!" instead of "I want THAT!" Her toys burst into brilliant colors. Duke Grumble complimented Count Compare's vest, and his feast came back vibrant and delicious. Even Captain Grumble-Beard's dull trinkets started gleaming gold.
"Take these," he said warmly, pressing a small golden COMPASS into each of their hooves and claws. "These point toward treasure. But I think you already know the real secret."
They raced home through the Whispering Wood, practicing the whole way: "I'm GLAD you're faster!" "I'm GLAD you remember the path!" Even the trees seemed brighter.
They burst through the cottage door just as Mom and Dad were reading baby Hazel a bedtime story.
Their old selves would have whined "NOT FAIR she gets a story!" Instead:
"Hazel's getting a story! That's so NICE!" Ivy said, and meant it.
"Can we help read?" Oliver added.
Soon all five were piled together — Ivy reading words, Oliver doing the funny voices, Dad doing the sound effects, Mom laughing, Hazel giggling at everything.
Their compasses spun wildly, then pointed straight at the family pile.
"Found it," they whispered together. "The best treasure of all."
